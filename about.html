About ToolsShare

Building stronger communities through tool sharing

Our Story

ToolsShare was born from a simple observation: most tools sit unused 95% of the time, while neighbors struggle with expensive purchases for occasional projects. We believed there had to be a better way.

Founded in 2023, our platform connects community members who want to share resources, save money, and reduce waste. What started as a local initiative has grown into a thriving network of tool enthusiasts, DIY homeowners, and professional contractors.

Our Mission

  • Accessibility: Make quality tools available to everyone, regardless of budget
  • Sustainability: Reduce waste by maximizing tool utilization
  • Community: Foster connections between neighbors and local makers
  • Empowerment: Enable people to tackle projects they never thought possible

Meet Our Team

Our Values

Trust & Safety

Every transaction is protected by our secure escrow system and comprehensive insurance coverage. We verify all users and maintain strict quality standards.

Environmental Impact

By sharing tools instead of buying new ones, our community has prevented over 2,000 tools from being manufactured, reducing carbon footprint significantly.

Community First

We prioritize local connections and fair pricing. Our platform is designed to strengthen neighborhoods, not extract value from them.

Directions

By Car

From downtown, take Main Street north for 2 miles. Turn right on Maker Street. We're in the blue building on the left with ample parking available.

By Public Transit

Take Bus Route 15 or 22 to the Maker District stop. Walk 2 blocks east on Maker Street. The hub is wheelchair accessible with ramp access.

By Bike

Bike racks available at the front entrance. We're on the city bike path network - follow the green signs to Maker District.
